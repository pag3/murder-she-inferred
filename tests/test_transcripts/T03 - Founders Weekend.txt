Jessica arrives at the historical society hall just after treasurer Arthur Bell is found slumped beside a locked display case. Deputy Mara Keene says the case contains a deed that could ruin several land deals. Felix Dunn, who chaired the festival, admits he argued with Arthur over missing donor money but claims he spent the fatal hour rehearsing on the bandstand with half the parade committee. Vivian Shore, the society president, says Arthur threatened to reveal that her family title to the inn was incomplete. She also insists she never entered the archive room, although her scarf pin turns up beneath the clerk's desk. Owen Pritchard says he drove Arthur home at ten, only to learn later that Arthur returned to the hall because someone called about the old deed. A waitress at the Wharf House remembers Owen ordering coffee there at 10:20, which keeps him away from the hall for part of the window, but not all of it. Jessica interviews the costume volunteers and learns Felix borrowed a master key ring for "decorations," then returned it with one key missing. [Band Music Continues] The archivist says Arthur planned to expose a fake donation ledger, and the forged entries would have embarrassed Vivian as well as Felix. [Crowd Cheering] Jessica finds that Vivian's scarf pin was bent weeks earlier during a quilting demonstration, which weakens the neat accusation against her. She also learns Owen's phone booth call to Arthur came from a gas station two towns over, timed after the body was already discovered, so Owen may be reckless but he is not the killer. When the marching band restages its route, Jessica realizes the bass drum from Felix's rehearsal hid the sound of the display case closing. She traces powdered plaster from the bandstand steps to Felix's shoes and finds the missing key taped inside his rolled festival banner. Felix breaks and admits Arthur caught him siphoning restoration funds; he lured Arthur back to the hall under the pretense of checking the deed, then staged the locked-case mystery to frame Vivian. Even so, Jessica notes that Vivian's lies about the title, Mara's impatience with Arthur, and Owen's desperate debts all made them plausible suspects until the timetable and key finally aligned.
