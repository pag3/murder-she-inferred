"Everybody loved him," says Isla Voss. "Well... everybody who wasn't about to be exposed." Marcus Reed laughs, then stops. "That's a nasty way to put it, isn't it?" He insists he only argued with the columnist about a bad review. Paula Kent asks, "If Marcus is innocent, why did he hide the revised brochure?" Marcus answers, "Because it looked terrible, not because I killed anyone." Isla says she spent the whole crossing calming seasick passengers; two deckhands agree, though one remembers she disappeared for "maybe thirty seconds... maybe a minute." Jessica rewinds the recorder and hears the victim say, "No, no, listen to me. If Paula knew about the false bookings, she'd walk." Paula fires back, "Wouldn't you, if your name was on the paperwork?" Then she adds, quieter, "I was in the galley from 8:40 until the horn sounded. Ask Chef Benoit. Ask anyone." Chef Benoit does ask, and confirms it. He also says Paula returned carrying a cup she never served. Marcus keeps repeating, "I told him to wait. I told him to wait," which sounds damning until Jessica learns he meant the printer proof, not the ferry departure. The real break comes when Isla says the lounge clock stopped at 8:52, yet Jessica notices the battery cover was unscrewed after the storm, meaning the clock cannot prove anything at all. A passenger remembers hearing Paula and the victim argue early, but the ship's purser places Paula on the lower deck when the fatal dose was poured. Jessica finally turns to Isla. "You wanted that interview stopped, didn't you?" Isla answers, "Wanted? Perhaps. Needed? That's another matter." Her umbrella tip matches the puncture in the poisoned thermos seal, and the fake sympathy in her first statement collapses the moment Jessica mentions the hidden booking ledger.
