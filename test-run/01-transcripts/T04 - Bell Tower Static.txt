Pastor Len Mercer says the victim had uncovered sabotage in the town emergency signal system. [Static Cracking] Doria Pike insists she was setting up folding chairs in the fellowship hall the entire evening, and three volunteers confirm seeing her there until the storm knocked out the lights. Theo Marsh says he raced up the tower because he heard shouting, but his shoes are wet with seawater rather than rainwater. [Thunder] "You cannot pin this on me," Theo snaps, even before Jessica asks about the broken frequency crystal in his coat. A choir member remembers hearing the victim say, "If Len sells the repeater plans, everybody will know," moments before the final bell. [Choir Rehearsal Continues] Len denies any sale, yet Jessica finds a draft contract in his briefcase and learns he met the buyer at lunch. [Footsteps on Wood] Doria's alibi grows stronger when the custodian produces a Polaroid of her hanging lanterns below the clock face at the key moment, but Theo's story keeps shifting between the dock, the tower, and the parking lot. [Static Squeal] Jessica discovers the seawater on Theo's shoes came from a bait cooler he kicked over while hiding the crystal behind hymnals. He had motive because the engineer planned to reveal that Theo had been skimming repair funds for months. [Lightning Pops] The church sexton says the storm surge knocked out the side door at 8:47, which matters because Theo had insisted the door stayed bolted all evening. Jessica checks the hinges and finds fresh paint scraped away, as though someone rushed heavy equipment through the frame. Len says that only proves the engineer had enemies beyond the church, but Jessica notices he never once asks who moved the equipment, only whether the repeater records were damaged. Doria recalls seeing the engineer argue with Len over a sealed envelope marked "Harbor Emergency Contract." Theo interrupts to say Doria is embellishing, then claims he was at the dock buying fuel. The dockmaster corrects him: Theo never reached the counter, because he left after kicking over a bait cooler when he saw a patrol car. [Choir Warms Up] Jessica interviews the choir director, who says the final bell was rung manually, not electronically, because the transmitter had been unstable for weeks. That means someone who knew the maintenance schedule also knew exactly when the tower would be empty except for the engineer. Len admits Theo had access to the maintenance notes, but he also had them himself. Jessica rewinds the evening in careful order. The volunteers place Doria in the fellowship hall hanging lanterns. A Polaroid confirms it. The sexton places Len in the vestry drafting a statement for the preservation board, though only until 8:40. Theo cannot decide whether he was at the dock, in the parking lot, or halfway up the stairs when he heard the shouting. [Static Bursts] In the engineer's notebook Jessica finds calculations for load balancing and a list of suspicious payments tied to replacement parts that were never installed. Several entries are initialed T.M., but one larger contract is linked to L.M. Len insists those initials mean "line maintenance." Jessica is not convinced. She checks the draft contract in his briefcase and learns Len had been quietly negotiating the sale of the church repeater plans to a private alarm company that would bypass the town system entirely. The engineer objected because the town would lose emergency coverage during storms. Doria says that explains motive for Len, but not the broken crystal in Theo's coat. Jessica replies that one motive does not cancel another. [Wind Slams Door] The youth choir remembers Theo stomping through rehearsal muttering that the engineer would "take food off my table." Theo finally admits he skimmed repair funds, but claims the engineer had agreed to stay quiet until the audit. The problem with that story, Jessica says, is the engineer had already mailed copies of the payment records to the town clerk. Theo swears he only wanted to steal the crystal to erase the maintenance logs, not to kill anyone. When Jessica searches the tower rafters with the sexton, she finds the missing backup battery wrapped in oilcloth and hidden above the hymnals. The cloth came from Len's office. Len says Theo must have planted it there. Theo says Len did it. Neither man notices Jessica staring at the ladder hooks. They are dry except for one rung stained with brine from the bait cooler. Whoever climbed last did so after the cooler spilled. [Bell Rope Sways] That timing matters. If Theo knocked over the cooler while hiding the crystal, he could not also have sabotaged the backup battery earlier in the evening unless he made two separate trips. The custodian remembers exactly that: first Len went up "to pray," then later Theo ran up after the shouting. Jessica reconstructs the tower sequence in front of everyone. Len met the engineer privately and pressured him to stop the audit. When the engineer refused, Len struck the repeater housing loose and sent the man stumbling against the beam. Realizing the fall might not look accidental once the payment records surfaced, Len hid the backup battery and left the draft contract behind. Theo then rushed up hoping to steal the crystal and protect his own embezzlement, accidentally coating his shoes in seawater from the cooler he had just kicked over downstairs. Theo's panic made him look murderous, but Doria's alibi and the timing of the battery sabotage narrow the actual killing to Len. [Thunder Rolls Away] Len tries to protest that the church needed the money, that no one understands how expensive the repairs were, that the engineer forced his hand by refusing compromise. Jessica tells him money troubles explain desperation, not murder. Doria adds that the emergency signal system served people who trusted the church to protect them in storms. Theo, chastened and cornered, confesses the skimming scheme anyway and says he assumed Len would blame him if the books came to light. Jessica notes that assumption nearly worked. The town already distrusted Theo's temper, and the crystal in his pocket was damning. But alibi quality, sequence of movements, and physical traces still matter more than appearance. By the next morning the chapel steps are crowded with reporters asking whether the emergency system ever worked at all. Jessica answers that it worked well enough to expose everybody who treated it as private property. [Static Fades] She watches the repair crew test the bells again and thinks about how often emergencies reveal the hidden economy beneath a polite town: who profits, who panics, and who assumes public duty can be sold off quietly in the dark.
